# ThinkRoot Parent Presentation
## Fresh Outline for Rebuilt Deck

Audience:
- Bay Area parents
- Highly educated, analytical, often software or technical professionals

Duration:
- 22 to 25 minutes speaking
- 5 minutes Q&A

## Core framing

This presentation should:
- wake parents up to the gap between academic success and real-life capability
- validate the pain points they are already seeing
- establish scientific credibility
- show that ThinkRoot is structured and practical
- make the 6-week program feel like the full developmental path
- position the 5-day program as a shorter, lower-commitment entry point into the same work

## Recommended opening direction

Primary hook:

**Your child may be doing well in school. But what happens when life does not go according to plan?**

Reason:
- immediate
- parent-relevant
- serious
- stronger than opening with metaphor

Optional supporting frame later:

**The mind can become a child's greatest strength. Or the thing that quietly limits their potential.**

## Slide flow

### Slide 1
**Title**
Building Children Who Can Handle Life

**Purpose**
Introduce the talk with a serious, credible tone.

### Slide 2
**Title**
Your child may be doing well in school. But what happens when life does not go according to plan?

**Purpose**
Wake-up slide. Shift attention from achievement to real-life response.

### Slide 3
**Title**
What parents are already seeing

**Purpose**
Recognition slide:
- panic before exams
- shutting down after mistakes
- fear of failure
- comparison
- low frustration tolerance

### Slide 4
**Title**
This is not a personality problem. It is a skills gap.

**Purpose**
Reframe the issue as teachable and practical.

### Slide 5
**Title**
The gap schools do not fill

**Purpose**
Show the difference between what children are taught and what they are rarely taught.

### Slide 6
**Title**
Why this matters now

**Purpose**
Raise urgency:
- pressure starts early
- comparison is constant
- external success can hide internal fragility

### Slide 7
**Title**
What the science says

**Purpose**
Establish credibility with selective evidence:
- self-efficacy
- emotional regulation
- resilience
- early pattern formation

### Slide 8
**Title**
What makes ThinkRoot different

**Purpose**
Differentiate ThinkRoot from generic coping or confidence programs.

**Standout line**
**ThinkRoot goes deeper — to the root cause.**

### Slide 9
**Title**
What children build through ThinkRoot

**Purpose**
Translate the work into outcomes parents value:
- judgment
- responsibility
- emotional steadiness
- confidence under challenge
- better recovery after setbacks

### Slide 10
**Title**
The full pathway: the 6-week program

**Purpose**
Show parents the architecture of the deeper program without overwhelming them.

**On-slide direction**
Six-session overview, one line each:
- Session 1: awareness of patterns
- Session 2: working with emotions and the body
- Session 3: thoughts, stories, and self-talk
- Session 4: shifting patterns in real time
- Session 5: practice, application, and responsibility
- Session 6: integration, confidence, and leadership direction

**Speaker-note rule**
Give enough detail for parents to feel substance, but keep it high-level and outcome-linked.

### Slide 11
**Title**
The 5-day Resilience Lab

**Purpose**
Position the 5-day program as the shorter, accessible starting point.

**On-slide direction**
- built from the same core philosophy
- more compact
- practical introduction
- strong first step for families who want an entry point

### Slide 12
**Title**
What students actually do

**Purpose**
Make the experience concrete.

**On-slide direction**
- guided reflection
- discussion
- structured exercises
- simple practical tools
- home practice / carryover

### Slide 13
**Title**
Why parents respond to this work

**Purpose**
Use proof and observable outcomes.

### Slide 14
**Title**
Why I created this work

**Purpose**
Support credibility through Tina's background and lived seriousness without making the deck biography-heavy.

### Slide 15
**Title**
Next steps

**Purpose**
Invite parents to express interest in either:
- the 5-day program now
- the 6-week program as the fuller offering
- bringing the program to a school/community if enough families are interested

## Tone rules

The deck should sound:
- scientific
- practical
- high-trust
- calm
- serious
- parent-readable

The deck should avoid:
- fluffy empowerment language
- therapy framing
- excessive curriculum detail on slides
- sounding anti-academics
