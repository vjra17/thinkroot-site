Slide 1
Building Children Who Can Handle Life

An introduction to ThinkRoot Young Leaders and Resilience Lab
Tina Puri | Founder, ThinkRoot Young Leaders

Slide 2
Many children today have strong hardware: intelligence, opportunity, and support. But under pressure, the internal patterns underneath often take over.

Success, structure, and opportunity matter. But they do not automatically build resilience, judgment, or inner strength.

Speaker Notes
I want to start with an image I once heard Warren Buffett use in a different context. He spoke about the difference between the wolf and the dog in a golden cage. One survives by staying sharp, adaptive, and alert. The other is protected, provided for, and comfortable, but over time loses some of the instincts it was born with. He was talking about work and independence. But I want to borrow that image for something else. Because I believe our children's minds can develop in a similar way. A mind that learns how to handle pressure, setbacks, uncertainty, and disappointment becomes stronger. It becomes steadier. It grows in capacity. A mind that is never taught what to do when life gets hard can remain outwardly successful, but inwardly fragile. So the real question is not only: Is my child doing well? It is: What kind of mind are we building underneath that success?

What limits many children is not poverty, lack of opportunity, or lack of intelligence. Your children have loving parents who have sacrificed enormously. They have tutors, activities, good schools, and resources. And yet many of them are still struggling in ways all of that cannot fully fix. Because the issue is internal. It is the thought pattern, the emotional habit, the automatic reaction that takes over when life gets hard. And most children, and most adults, are never directly taught how to work with that.

Slide 11
AAA — the structured method behind ThinkRoot
StepWhat it meansWhy it matters
A — AwarenessNotice what is happening in the mind and body before it escalates. Catch the thought, feeling, or physical signal early.This is the step most children have never been taught.
A — AcceptAcknowledge the thought or feeling without fighting it, suppressing it, or pushing it away.Suppression often strengthens patterns. Acceptance makes change possible. This is one of the places ThinkRoot differs from generic coping advice.
A — ActionUse a specific, practised technique to redirect and choose a steadier response.The Five Power Technique is the concrete Action tool: under 3 minutes, no equipment, memorised and usable before a test, after a mistake, or at bedtime.
ThinkRoot goes deeper — to the root cause.
