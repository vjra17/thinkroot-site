## SLIDE 7 — The Science

**Headline:**
This is not a theory. The science is clear.

**On-slide research blocks:**
- **Self-efficacy matters:** what a child believes about their ability affects how they perform, persist, and recover.
- **Emotional regulation affects cognition:** stress and emotional overload reduce focus, memory, and decision-making.
- **Patterns become habits:** what children repeat internally becomes easier to repeat over time.
- **Human skills matter long-term:** resilience, judgment, adaptability, and self-awareness shape adult success as much as academics do.

**Purpose of slide:**
Establish scientific credibility without overwhelming the audience.

**Speaker notes:**
For a highly analytical audience, this cannot just sound inspiring. It has to be grounded.

Research across psychology, education, and development tells us several things clearly. First, self-efficacy matters deeply. What a child believes about their ability affects how they approach challenge.

Second, emotional overload affects cognition. A stressed child does not think as clearly, remember as well, or decide as wisely.

Third, repeated internal patterns become habits. If children keep practicing panic, avoidance, or harsh self-talk, those patterns do not simply disappear on their own.

And finally, the so-called human skills that adults need later, resilience, judgment, adaptability, are not optional extras. They shape outcomes.

## SLIDE 8 — Why Early Matters

**Headline:**
Patterns that are not addressed early do not simply disappear

**On-slide points:**
- They become easier to repeat
- They begin shaping behavior and relationships
- They affect confidence and decision-making
- They become harder to change later

**Bottom line:**
Childhood is the easier window for building better patterns.

**Purpose of slide:**
Make the timing argument clear.

**Speaker notes:**
This is why I care so much about childhood.

The earlier we help children recognize patterns, understand them, and build healthier responses, the easier that work is.

If we wait until those patterns are deeply reinforced, change becomes much harder. That is why this work is not about fixing a crisis. It is about building strength at the right time.

## SLIDE 11 — The Full Pathway

**Headline:**
The 6-week program: the fuller developmental path

**On-slide session overview:**
- **Session 1:** noticing patterns
- **Session 2:** understanding emotions and the body
- **Session 3:** thoughts, stories, and self-talk
- **Session 4:** shifting patterns in real time
- **Session 5:** practicing new responses with responsibility
- **Session 6:** integration, confidence, and leadership direction

**Bottom line:**
This is the fuller architecture of the work.

**Purpose of slide:**
Show parents the deeper structure without becoming curriculum-heavy.

**Speaker notes:**
For parents who want to know what the fuller program actually looks like, this is the high-level path.

It is designed as a developmental sequence, not a collection of disconnected activities.

Children first learn to notice patterns. Then they understand what is happening emotionally and physically. Then they learn how thoughts and interpretations shape experience. From there, they begin practicing how to interrupt and shift those patterns. Then they apply that work with more responsibility and maturity. And finally, they integrate what they have learned into a stronger sense of confidence and direction.

So the six-week version is the fuller path.
