Why It Matters

Your child’s brain is their most powerful asset. Are you training it?

We invest in math tutors, coding camps, and sports coaches. We train every skill our children will need — except one. The skill of managing their own mind.

Your child has up to 80,000 thoughts every day. Most are automatic. Many are negative — not because your child is negative, but because that is how the human brain is wired. And those thoughts are quietly shaping how they perform, relate to others, and feel about themselves.

The science is clear: unmanaged negative thought patterns in childhood don’t disappear. They accumulate — shaping behavior, relationships, and health for decades.

They do not stay in the mind alone. Over time, they shape emotional states, stress responses, sleep, behavior, and the way a child experiences the world around them.

Most adults are still running on thought patterns formed before age ten.

The best time to build these skills is now — Before the patterns harden. Before the pressure intensifies. Before the baggage gets heavy.

What makes ThinkRoot different

Most programs work at the surface. ThinkRoot works at the root.

Most programs help children manage behavior in the moment. ThinkRoot goes deeper - to the root cause. It helps children work with the patterns that drive behavior in the first place: stress responses, self-talk, emotional habits, and the meaning they make of difficulty.

That is why the shift goes beyond one difficult moment. Children recover more quickly, respond with more clarity, and build lifelong skills that strengthen school performance, relationships, judgment, and resilience.

Meet the founder

Tina Puri

Founder & Program Director

Tina Puri is a Certified Wellness Educator and Senior Peer Counselor — certified by the cities of Fremont, Union City, and Newark — with more than a decade of experience working directly with children, adults, and seniors across all age groups and settings.

She delivered mindfulness, stress management, and gratitude programs at the high school level — among the first of their kind on campus — and continues to teach values-based character development to elementary and middle school students, while also teaching seniors. She also mentored HOSA chapters at both middle and high school levels, supporting students through statewide and nationwide competitions, and served as a Stanford Challenge Success volunteer.

A software professional with 20+ years of experience, she brings both scientific rigor and deep practical teaching experience to ThinkRoot programs.

"I teach this because it changed my life. After more than twenty years in software, my body gave out — fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue, severe anxiety. I went from being bedridden to fully well again. That transformation came through the same root-level work I now teach children.

I am not teaching this because I read about it. I am teaching it because I know how deeply it can change a life."
