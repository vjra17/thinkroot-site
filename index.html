Real stories

What actually happened — in their own lives.

These are not hypothetical outcomes. These are real children who completed ThinkRoot programs and what their parents and teachers noticed after.

From struggling in math to mentoring her classmates
One student came in with a growing belief that she simply wasn't a math person. By learning to recognize the thoughts that were limiting her — and practice transforming them — her relationship with the subject shifted entirely. Her scores improved. Then something unexpected happened: she started helping other students who were struggling with the same thing.
Upper elementary
She became the person her classmates turned to in conflict
When disagreements broke out among her peers, one student naturally started stepping in — not to take sides, but to help everyone feel heard. She wasn't assigned this role. She grew into it. Her parents noticed she wasn't just handling her own emotions better — she was genuinely helping others navigate theirs.
Middle school
She started a daily practice — without being asked
The clearest sign that a skill has truly landed is when a child uses it outside of class, without prompting. One student began meditating on her own, every day. Her parents saw changes across every part of her life — how she handled stress, how she slept, how she showed up in her family. She chose this. No one told her to.
Upper elementary
She turned off the TV — and didn't look back
One student had developed a habit of watching TV for hours each day — something that was quietly affecting her sleep, focus, and mood. After the program she reduced it significantly, on her own. Her academic performance improved. Her social confidence improved. Her emotional wellbeing improved. The change came from inside — not from being told.

The science

Measurable results. Decades of research.

ThinkRoot teaches children one practical skill: how to notice a thought before it takes over — and what to do next. Simple, repeatable, works anywhere. The outcomes are well established across decades of research in child development:

Academic focus and performance
Improved attention, reduced distraction, better academic outcomes at school
💪

Meet the founder

Tina Puri

Founder & Program Director

Tina Puri is a Certified Wellness Educator and Senior Peer Counselor — certified by the cities of Fremont, Union City, and Newark. With more than a decade working directly with children, adults, and seniors, she has delivered mindfulness and gratitude programs at the high school level, mentored HOSA students through statewide competitions, and served as a Stanford Challenge Success volunteer. A software professional for 20+ years, she brings both scientific rigor and deep practical teaching experience to ThinkRoot.

She and her son co-founded Rewired to Inspire (2016–2020), a free intergenerational community program at Fremont Main Library. Together they mentored student volunteers from Mission San Jose and Irvington High Schools to design and deliver sessions connecting youth with adults 55+. The program ran for four years, was featured in Tri-City Voice, and received a letter of recognition from Congressman Michael M. Honda.

"I teach this because it changed my life. After more than twenty years in software, my body gave out — fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue, severe anxiety. Working at the root level, with the same practices I now teach children, resolved what traditional medicine could only manage. I am not teaching this because I read about it. I am teaching it because it is why I am standing here."
