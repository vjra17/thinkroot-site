"Schools teach reading, writing, and arithmetic.
But who teaches kids how to manage their own mind?"

The Gap

Your child is being taught everything except how to think.

Every child carries a remarkable inner world — thoughts, emotions, self-beliefs — that shapes everything from their grades to their friendships to their confidence. Yet no one teaches them how it works.

Anxiety and self-doubt that no one has given them tools to address
No framework for handling tough situations — conflicts, pressure, big emotions
Freedom from overthinking and the negative loops that drain their energy
A growing sense that feelings are something they can work with — not just survive
Program 1 — Resilience Lab

Five days. One complete
resilience foundation.

One week. One hour a day. Monday through Friday.

Grades 2–4 · Ages 7–10 Grades 5–8 · Ages 11–14 5 Days · 1 hr/day · Mon–Fri Small Groups

Each session builds on the previous day — a complete arc from first noticing thoughts to actively transforming them. By Friday, your child has a working toolkit they can use any time.

01
Meet Your Brain
"You are not your thoughts. You are the one noticing them."
02
Thought Weather
"Thoughts come in two kinds — sunshine and storm. Both pass."
03
The Feeling Body
"Your body carries every feeling. Learn to read its signals."
04
Kindness is a Superpower
"One sunshine thought changes your brain chemistry."
05
I Am the Author
"You have more influence over your inner world than anyone told you."
What they take home
Personal Resilience Toolkit — built on Day 5
Daily Practice Cards — one per day
The Five Power Technique — memorized, usable any time

Meet your facilitator
Tina Puri
Certified Wellness Educator
Senior Peer Counselor
Founder, ThinkRoot Young Leaders

Tina Puri is a Certified Wellness Educator and Senior Peer Counselor with more than a decade of experience working directly with children, adults, and seniors across all age groups and settings. She delivered yoga, mindfulness, and gratitude programs at the high school level — among the first programs of their kind on campus — and has taught values-based character development to elementary and middle school students.


She mentored students through HOSA chapters at both middle and high school levels, supporting their preparation for statewide and nationwide competitions. She served as a parent volunteer for Stanford University's Challenge Success program, accompanying students to conferences at Stanford with leading researchers in student wellbeing and resilience. She has also led wellness sessions for senior communities and adults.


A software professional with 20+ years of experience, she brings both scientific rigor and deep practical teaching experience to ThinkRoot programs.
