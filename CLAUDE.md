## Target Audience

Busy, achievement-oriented Bay Area parents — many are software professionals. They:
- Prioritise academics and sports over wellness
- Have limited time and will leave if the page feels dense or theoretical
- Respond to practical outcomes, credibility signals, and efficiency
- Are NOT the audience for vague wellness language or spiritual framing

---

## Brand & Positioning

- **Core idea:** Self-intelligence — the missing skill layer beneath academics, sports, and confidence
- **Feel:** Practical, scientific, credible, efficient — NOT therapeutic, spiritual, or vague
- **Differentiator:** ThinkRoot goes to the root cause (stress responses, self-talk, emotional habits, stories children tell themselves about difficulty) — not just coping skills
- **Launch model:** Priority Interest List (not direct enroll/pay) — groups form by grade, location, schedule
- **Primary CTA:** "Join the Priority Interest List"

---

## Homepage Section Order & Purpose

1. **Hero** — name the missing skill, connect to outcomes parents already care about
2. **Gap** — make parents recognise the problem (CDC 42%, Harvard 4/5 stats)
3. **Why It Matters** — raise stakes, show how missing layer affects everything
4. **What Makes ThinkRoot Different** — not SEL, not coping, goes to root cause
5. **How It Works** — Build / Practice / Apply (repeatable process)
6. **Resilience Lab Programs** — 5-Day Intensive + 6-Week Series (current offering)
7. **Real Stories** — 5 real student outcomes with school-level attribution
8. **Leadership Lab** — Coming Soon teaser
9. **About Tina** — founder credibility, practical + scientific
10. **Community / Rewired to Inspire** — proof of concept, future pathway
11. **Interest Form** — low-friction priority list signup
